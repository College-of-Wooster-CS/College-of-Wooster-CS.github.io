Research & Internships

We encourage all of our students to find opportunities to apply their skills to a research opportunity or internship. Below includes a number of possibilities where students can find such positions.

In addition to searching via google, LinkedIn, or other search engines, students can use the college's Advising, Planning, and Experiential Learning Center (APEX) to help them find an internship. If the internship is unpaid or underpaid, APEX also offers competitive fellowships to help supplement a stipend for the student. As we are contacted by businesses or potential employers, we also post opportunities regularly to the Opportunities Channel of the Mathematical and Computational Sciences Microsoft Teams organization. A student can also opt to work with a faculty mentor in order to count their internship for academic credit (0.25, 0.5, or 1 credit depending on the number of hours worked).

Over the summer, the college offers a competitive Applied Methods and Research Experience (AMRE) program in which a group of students are paired with faculty mentors to consult for a business, industry, or agency client to solve real-world problems. There is an open call for applications in the spring on Microsoft Teams.

Finally, we have a number of research opportunities available to students. The Sophomore Research Program allows students to work with a faculty advisor on a specific research project during the Fall, Spring, or Summer. Students can also apply to any off-campus location as found in the U.S. National Science Foundation Research Experiences for Undergraduates (NSF REU) to explore other summer research opportunities.
